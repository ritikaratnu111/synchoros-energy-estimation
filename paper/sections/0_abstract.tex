\begin{abstract}
Coarse-Grained Reconfigurable Arrays (CGRAs) are promising as accelerators for energy and performance benefits.
Agile and accurate energy estimates are essential to explore the CGRA design space and guide algorithm mapping decisions.
%Design efficient CGRAs, requires exploring the CGRA design space for variations in the desired functionality, dimensions and parallelism.
%often requires multiple iterations, where each iteration needs accurate energy estimations for making informed design choices.
Existing estimations are agile but inaccurate since they do not factor in the impact of wires in the design. 
This paper presents \textit{synchoros} energy estimation methodology for post-route accurate estimations.
The methodology leverages the properties of synchoros VLSI design style to incorporate the impact of interconnects in a CGRA design.
%This paper presents SEMA, an energy estimation methodology at the algorithm-level that models the impact of wires by leveraging the properties of \emph{synchoros} VLSI design style.
Overall, our methodology achieves 98\% estimation accuracy relative to post-route results for algorithms irrespective of variations in functionality, dimensions, and parallelism. 
\end{abstract}
